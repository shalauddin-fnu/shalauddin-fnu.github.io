Experimental AMO Physics

Research

My research is in experimental atomic, molecular, and optical physics, with an emphasis on ultrafast molecular dynamics. At the James R. Macdonald Laboratory, I employ femtosecond pump–probe spectroscopy and cold-target recoil-ion momentum spectroscopy (COLTRIMS) to investigate molecular ionization, fragmentation, and structural dynamics.

Current PhD Research

My initial PhD project focuses on the ultrafast dynamics of methyl iodide (CH3I), investigated using a two-color femtosecond pump–probe experiment. The experimental platform is based on a Ti:sapphire laser system operating at a repetition rate of 10 kHz, with pulse energies of up to approximately 2 mJ and pulse durations of approximately 30 fs.

Research Communication

I have presented this research to the broader AMO physics community through an oral presentation at a meeting of the American Physical Society Division of Atomic, Molecular and Optical Physics (APS DAMOP) and a poster presentation at a Gordon Research Conference.
