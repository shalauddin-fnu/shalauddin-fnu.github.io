Research and learning area

Molecular Dynamics & Coulomb Explosion

This section develops the physical ideas behind molecular fragmentation and Coulomb explosion imaging. It connects molecular structure, ionization, momentum conservation, kinetic-energy release, and potential-energy surfaces with the experimental observables used to investigate ultrafast molecular dynamics.

Learning themes

The material in this collection is organized around the physical concepts needed to interpret molecular fragmentation experiments.

THEME 01

Molecular Structure and Dynamics

Molecular geometry, electronic states, nuclear motion, potential-energy surfaces, and characteristic molecular timescales.

THEME 02

Ionization and Fragmentation

Strong-field ionization, charge localization, dissociation pathways, and the physical origin of molecular fragments.

THEME 03

Momentum and Energy Imaging

Momentum conservation, kinetic-energy release, angular correlations, and reconstruction of molecular structure from fragment momenta.

Research notes and articles

This collection organizes theoretical explanations, equations, references, and experimental connections used in the study of Coulomb explosion and ultrafast molecular dynamics.

Foundations of Coulomb Explosion Imaging

The starting point for this collection is the relationship between molecular geometry and the momenta of charged fragments. The notes in this area connect the underlying physical model with the quantities measured in a momentum-imaging experiment.
